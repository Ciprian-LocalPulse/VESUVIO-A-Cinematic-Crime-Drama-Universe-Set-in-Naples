# Production Bible — VFX

## Philosophy

Minimal, invisible VFX in service of grounded realism — no stylized or
graphic-novel-style effects work. VFX budget is prioritized for:

- **Environment extension/cleanup** of Naples locations (removing modern
  signage, extending period-appropriate rione blocks, etc.)
- **Subtle Vesuvius enhancement** — the faint glowing "vein" motif
  established in the Pilot script (Scene: Ranieri Villa dinner) requires
  restrained, near-subliminal compositing rather than an overt effects
  sequence.
- **Safety-driven violence work** — muzzle flashes, blood work, and
  impact effects handled digitally where needed to keep on-set violence
  brief and controlled, consistent with the Story Bible's approach to
  violence (Section 11).

## What to Avoid

Any VFX that calls attention to itself. The show's visual identity
depends on the audience never doubting they're watching real Naples.
