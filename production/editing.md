# Production Bible — Editing

## Pacing Philosophy
Deliberate, patient pacing in line with prestige-drama conventions —
scenes are allowed to breathe; the show trusts silence and stillness as
much as incident.

## Structural Rhythm
- Cold opens favor a single striking image (often Vesuvius or a
  character in isolation) over expository dialogue.
- Act breaks land on emotional decisions, not just plot incidents.
- Parallel-action cross-cutting (see Pilot Act Four) used sparingly, for
  season-defining beats rather than routine scene transitions.

## Continuity Notes
Maintain a consistent, season-long visual "temperature" — Season 1 should
read as a single sustained escalation from composure to rupture, visible
in cut rhythm as much as story.
