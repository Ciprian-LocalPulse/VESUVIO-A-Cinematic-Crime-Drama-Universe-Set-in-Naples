# VESUVIO: Blood of Naples
## Pitch Deck

*An original idea by Ciprian Stefan Plesca*

---

## Logline

For ten years, an uneasy peace has held the criminal underworld of Naples
together. When a single act of betrayal breaks it, three generations of
one family — bound by blood, honor, and the mountain that watches over
them — are forced to choose between loyalty to the past and survival in
the present.

## Format

Modern crime drama series. Season 1: 10 episodes x ~50 minutes.
Multi-season arc planned (3 seasons minimum).

## Tone

Grounded, character-driven prestige drama. Restrained violence, high
emotional and political stakes. Closer in spirit to modern
family-crime-saga television than genre pastiche — Naples as a living
character, not a backdrop.

## Audience

Adult drama audiences (18-49 core) who follow prestige crime and family-
saga television; strong crossover appeal for international audiences via
subtitled/dubbed distribution, given the strength of Italian-language
crime drama in the global streaming market.

## References

For tonal and structural reference (not derivative intent):
- Prestige multi-generational crime family dramas
- Regional-language European crime series with global streaming success
- Character-first ensemble writing over plot-first genre execution

## Why Now

Regional-language crime drama has proven, repeatedly, that authentic
setting and dialect are a strength, not a barrier, for global streaming
audiences. Naples — a city with a globally recognizable visual and
cultural identity, and an underexplored one on premium television — is a
natural next setting for this genre. The project arrives with story,
music, and visual identity already built, reducing early development
risk for a partner studio.

## Style

- **Visual:** Naturalistic, high-contrast night photography; Vesuvius as
  a recurring visual motif; muted, desaturated palette with restrained
  gold accents (see [`/docs/Moodbook.md`](../docs/Moodbook.md)).
- **Sound:** Original Neapolitan-dialect theme and score blending
  traditional motifs with modern, dark melodic production (see
  [`/assets/track_final.mp3`](../assets/track_final.mp3)).
- **Language:** Dialogue in Neapolitan dialect and Italian, subtitled —
  prioritizing authenticity over accessibility compromises.
