## OFFICIAL WORLD BIBLE — EXTENDED EDITION

*"'A cetta' nun perdona, ma nun se scorda maje."*
*The city doesn't forgive, but it never forgets.*

---

### A note on this document

The Story Bible establishes what is true about the VESUVIO universe at the level of theme, politics, and family. The Character Bible establishes who lives inside it. This World Bible exists to answer the question a location scout, a production designer, an assistant director building a shooting schedule, and a writer blocking a scene all ask next: **where, exactly, are we, what does it look like, who else is in the room, and what are the rules of this specific place?**

This document is organized in six parts:

- **Part One**: The city, mapped — districts, geography, and territorial control.
- **Part Two**: Full location catalogue, expanding Story Bible §18.
- **Part Three**: Institutions of the world — beyond the named characters, the organizations and offices that shape the story.
- **Part Four**: Rules of the universe — logistics, economics, and constraints a writer needs to avoid continuity errors.
- **Part Five**: The calendar — how the in-world year moves, ritually and seasonally.
- **Part Six**: The extended world — minor recurring characters, background texture, and the world beyond Naples.

# PART ONE — THE CITY, MAPPED

---

# 1. Naples: Territorial Overview

VESUVIO's Naples is built on the Story Bible's foundational split between **Napoli di Sopra** and **Napoli di Sotto** (Story Bible §4), but a working writers'-room and production map needs more resolution than that binary. This World Bible divides the city into five functional zones. None of these are real, precisely-mapped Naples districts reproduced 1:1 — per Story Bible §5's authenticity boundary, they are original composite geographies built to feel true to Naples' actual density, verticality, and contradictions without claiming documentary specificity about any real address or block.

| Zone | Controlled By | Function in the Story |
|---|---|---|
| **The Rione** | Famiglia Ranieri | Home turf; emotional center; where most family-scene episodes begin and end |
| **Esposito Territory** | Clan Esposito | Younger, newer, visually distinct; the show's "new Naples" |
| **The Historic Center** | Nominally no one; functionally the state and tourist economy | Napoli di Sopra; institutional and legitimate-business scenes |
| **The Port District** | Ranieri (contracts) / contested | Economic engine; large-scale set-pieces; night-shift world |
| **Contested & Neutral Ground** | Varies, unstable | Where the Peace's rules are tested most directly; site of the inciting incident |

---

# 2. The Rione (Ranieri Territory)

**Character of the place:** Dense mid-century housing blocks arranged around narrow, sloped streets that were never built for cars; laundry strung between buildings at every level; ground-floor shopfronts that have been in the same families for generations; wall shrines to the dead (a real and visually central Neapolitan tradition, treated with respect and specificity per Story Bible §12) at nearly every corner.

**Sub-locations within the Rione:**
- **Piazza Sant'Antonio** (proposed name) — the neighborhood's real civic heart; where funerals process, where the Ranieri family is seen in public, where news travels fastest.
- **Via dei Mille Passi** (proposed name, ironic — "the street of a thousand steps," reflecting its steep grade) — the residential street where Domenico and Assunta's family home sits, deliberately unshowy from the outside.
- **The Bar Vittoria** — the neighborhood's informal information exchange; old men, coffee, the rhythm of daily Rione life; a location that should recur as texture even in episodes with no plot business there.
- **The covered market (mercato coperto)** — a location for casual, "unofficial" meetings that need to look incidental; good for scenes where two characters need to talk without being seen to be meeting.

**Design mandate:** the Rione should never be shot to look poor in a way that reads as pitiable — it should read as *dense, alive, and proud*, per Story Bible §3's sensory palette. Poverty and dignity coexist here without contradiction; the production design and camera department should resist any impulse to aestheticize deprivation.

---

# 3. Esposito Territory

**Character of the place:** Visually and texturally distinct from the Rione by deliberate design (Story Bible §18) — newer construction (much of it post-earthquake reinforcement-era building, giving it a harder, more concrete-forward look), more visible street-art and graffiti culture, younger crowds visible at all hours, more smartphone and social-media presence bleeding into the physical world (phone-lit faces at night, delivery mopeds, visible wifi/router clutter on balconies).

**Sub-locations:**
- **The old textile warehouse** — repurposed as an informal Esposito hub; should read as "new money finding a use for old infrastructure," a visual metaphor for the faction itself.
- **The rooftop network** — a proposed recurring visual/logistical device: Esposito's younger affiliates move across rooftops and interior courtyards in ways police and rival territory cannot easily follow, a texture that differentiates chase/pursuit sequences here from anything in the Rione.
- **The car wash front** — a legitimate-facing business used for informal meetings, contrasting with the Ranieri family's restaurant front (Story Bible §18) — the show's second "membrane" location, deliberately more downmarket and less established.

**Design mandate:** Esposito territory should never simply look "worse" than the Rione — it should look *hungrier*: newer money, less patience, less generational polish, but real and growing energy.

---

# 4. The Historic Center & Napoli di Sopra

**Character of the place:** Restored Baroque and Renaissance facades, functioning municipal infrastructure, a heavier police presence that is largely ceremonial in effect, EU-funded restoration project signage, tourist foot traffic. This is the zone where Story Bible §4's "performed normalcy" is most visually legible — every scene here should carry an awareness that its calm is provisional and, for the audience, ironic.

**Sub-locations:**
- **The Tribunale (Courthouse)** — Renata Colombo's primary location; see Part Three.
- **The Municipal Council chambers** — used sparingly, for Level 3 (Story Bible §8) political-pressure scenes.
- **Bianca's hospital or the Tribunale annex (pending Character Bible Appendix C resolution of her profession)** — the show's most consistent legitimate-world anchor location.
- **The upscale restaurant district** — where Level 2 "legitimate business" meetings (Story Bible §8) between the Ranieri family and non-criminal partners take place, distinct from the Ranieri-owned restaurant front in the Rione.

---

# 5. The Port District

**Character of the place:** Industrial scale — cranes, shipping containers stacked in canyons, night-shift sodium lighting, the specific sound-world of large machinery and distant ship horns. Per Story Bible §18, this location's scale should visually dwarf characters to communicate the real size of what's economically at stake.

**Sub-locations:**
- **The main container terminal** — Ranieri-controlled contract territory; site of the show's largest-scale set-pieces.
- **The old fish market wharf** — a smaller, older section of the port, still operating, used for more intimate scenes that need the port's texture without its industrial scale.
- **The customs office** — an institutional-pressure location, where Level 3 relationships (Story Bible §8) with port authority officials are dramatized.

---

# 6. Contested & Neutral Ground

**Function:** the show needs locations where neither family has home-turf advantage, for scenes that require genuine tension about who controls the room. Proposed anchor locations:

- **The cemetery** (also catalogued in Story Bible §18) — functionally neutral by social convention; both families bury their dead in overlapping ground, and funeral-etiquette scenes here carry unique tension because the code's usual territorial rules are suspended by mutual, unspoken agreement.
- **The church** — neutral by Don Peppino's decades-long moral authority (Character Bible §13) rather than by geography.
- **The stretch of coastline between Rione and Esposito territory** — proposed as the specific site of the Season 1 inciting incident (Story Bible §6), given its symbolic value as literally contested ground between the two territories, with the sea (Story Bible §19, glossary: *'o mare*) as a witness.

# 7. Primary Recurring Locations (Full Detail)

*(Expands the summary table in Story Bible §18 with production-level detail. Each entry includes narrative function, sensory notes, and a "who we typically see here" character list for scheduling reference.)*

**The Family Villa**
- *Function:* Private, "sacred" Ranieri space where the family's rules are absolute (Story Bible §18).
- *Detail:* Should feel simultaneously opulent and unsettled — a fortress that has stopped feeling safe as the season progresses. Proposed design note: the villa's security measures (cameras, staff, a gate) should become visually more prominent episode to episode, a silent visual tracker of the family's growing sense of threat.
- *Typically present:* Domenico, Assunta, Michele, Bianca (when home), Gennaro, Rocco (frequent guest), Don Peppino (for family occasions).

**The Restaurant Front**
- *Function:* Prestanome-fronted business (Story Bible §9, §18) used for entertaining and laundering.
- *Detail:* Warm and inviting on its public face; the back office is the show's clearest single-location symbol of the legitimate/illegitimate membrane. Proposed addition: a specific back booth, always kept "reserved," that functions as the show's recurring meeting table for Level 2 business (Story Bible §8).
- *Typically present:* Domenico, Rocco, Michele, occasional Level 2/3 institutional guests.

**The Church**
- *Function:* Ritual, confession, funerals, uneasy morality (Story Bible §12, §18).
- *Detail:* Candlelight, incense, the confessional as a recurring intimate two-hander set. Proposed addition: a side chapel with a specific wall shrine that recurs as a visual anchor for grief-processing scenes distinct from the main confessional setup.
- *Typically present:* Don Peppino, rotating — this location's whole function is that everyone eventually passes through it.

**The Cemetery**
- *Function:* Funerals, private grief, generational memory; a barometer of cost across the season (Story Bible §18).
- *Detail:* Proposed design note: track this location's visual "fullness" (fresh flowers, new stones, mourners present) as a season-long device — it should look different, and more populated with recent grief, in episode 10 than in episode 1.
- *Typically present:* Varies by episode; the location that most frequently brings both families into proximity under truce-etiquette.

**The Courthouse**
- *Function:* Institutional Fringe scenes; where the state's pressure becomes visible and specific (Story Bible §18).
- *Detail:* Fluorescent, procedural — an intentional tonal cold shower after Camorra-world scenes.
- *Typically present:* Renata Colombo, occasional Sarno, occasional Bianca (pending profession resolution).

# 9. One-Off & Episodic Locations (Design Framework)

For locations needed for single episodes or specific plot beats (a specific municipal office, a specific shop targeted for extortion, a specific safehouse), the following design framework should be applied for consistency, rather than a fixed additional list — this keeps the World Bible a living document rather than a constraint on Season Bible/episode-level creativity:

1. **Identify the zone** (Part One) the location falls within.
2. **Match its visual register** to that zone's established design mandate.
3. **Log it** in a running production location database (maintained outside this document, in the `production/` bible) once used, to prevent continuity conflicts across episodes and seasons.

# PART THREE — INSTITUTIONS

---

# 10. The Questura (Police Headquarters)

Antonio Sarno's professional base (Character Bible §11). Functions in the story primarily as a location for scenes that dramatize the gap between his two registers (Character Bible §14) — official briefing-room language versus the Rione dialect he reverts to under pressure. Proposed recurring minor characters: a younger, more idealistic colleague of Sarno's who has not yet learned to compartmentalize the way he has (a useful device for externalizing Sarno's internal conflict in dialogue, per the same technique used for Rocco/Domenico in the Character Bible).

---

# 11. The Tribunale (Courthouse) & Judicial System

Renata Colombo's institutional base (Character Bible §12). The World Bible proposes the judicial system be dramatized primarily through **process** rather than **verdict** across Season 1 — wiretap authorizations, asset-seizure hearings, witness-protection logistics — consistent with Story Bible §7's guidance that this pressure should register as a slow-building background force. A single recurring prosecutorial colleague or investigative team (unnamed at this canon stage, Appendix C) is proposed to give Colombo scene partners without requiring additional named principal cast.

---

# 12. The Church, Beyond Don Peppino

The Rione parish operates with the texture of a real, functioning Neapolitan Catholic community beyond its central priest character — an unnamed but visually consistent congregation, a sacristan, an altar-serving tradition that should visually include children from both the Ranieri and Esposito extended orbits without comment, reinforcing Story Bible §12's point that the church is one of the few spaces where the two families' children exist in genuine proximity.

---

# 13. Municipal & Regional Politics

Per Story Bible §8 (Level 3) and §17, the show's political-institutional world is dramatized through a small number of recurring but lower-tier figures — a district councilman dependent on Ranieri "problem-solving" for his own political survival, proposed as a recurring but non-principal character — rather than through named high-level political figures, keeping the show's focus on the family drama rather than expanding into political-thriller territory the Story Bible explicitly positions VESUVIO as distinct from (Story Bible §2, comparison to *Suburra*).

---

# 14. The Port Authority & Legitimate Business Ecosystem

Expands Story Bible §13's economic channels into specific institutional texture: a Port Authority customs official (proposed recurring minor character, Appendix C) who represents Channel 1's Level 3 institutional relationship in a personal, dramatizable form; a construction-firm partner or rival (proposed recurring minor character) who represents Channel 2's competitive and reputational pressures.

---

# 15. Media & Public Perception

VESUVIO's world includes a functioning contemporary media environment — local Naples news coverage of violence and municipal corruption, and, critically, social media as a modern pressure on the code of silence (Story Bible §11, §20) that the founding generation never had to manage. This should be dramatized primarily through its effect on younger characters (Gennaro, Esposito's younger affiliates) rather than through named journalist characters at this stage — a phone camera at the wrong moment is a more dangerous device, dramatically, in this world than a reporter with a byline.

# PART FOUR — RULES OF THE UNIVERSE

---

# 16. Economic Logistics (Expanding Story Bible §13)

For internal writers'-room consistency (not for on-screen "how-to" dramatization, consistent with Story Bible §13's guidance):

- **Cash flow cadence:** territorial extortion (*'o pizzo*) is collected on a recurring, predictable schedule known informally throughout the Rione — its predictability is part of what makes a refusal to pay (a classic B/C-plot engine, Story Bible §13) dramatically legible as a deliberate act rather than a misunderstanding.
- **Contract cycles:** Port and construction contracts (Channels 1–2) operate on real-world-plausible municipal and commercial tender cycles — annual/multi-year renewals — giving the Season Bible natural season-length pressure points (a contract renewal deadline is a strong season-spine mechanical device).
- **The Prestanome relationship:** front-business owners (Story Bible §9) receive a modest, regular payment for the use of their name and premises, separate from and smaller than the laundering flows that pass through their books — a distinction useful for writing Prestanome characters as economically dependent without being wealthy.

---

# 17. Communication & Surveillance Rules

- **No character in this world uses direct, explicit language about criminal activity over phone or text** — consistent with real documented practice and with the show's *'o silenzio* code (Story Bible §11); coded language, indirect reference, and in-person meetings are the default, and any deviation from this by a character should register as a meaningful lapse (carelessness, youth, panic), not a norm.
- **Wiretap/surveillance awareness** is universal among principal characters over a certain seniority — Domenico, Rocco, and Fabiana in particular should never be written as naive about the possibility of being overheard.
- **Gennaro's generation** is the show's designated pressure point for this rule — his comfort with digital communication, texting, and social media is a specific, trackable vulnerability distinct from his elders' caution (see Character Bible §8).

---

# 18. Legal & Procedural Constraints

For narrative plausibility (not legal-procedural accuracy for its own sake): Italian criminal procedure generally allows longer pre-trial investigative and detention periods than US procedure, and organized-crime prosecutions specifically often rely on *pentiti* (collaborating witnesses, Story Bible §10) as a central evidentiary mechanism rather than direct physical evidence alone — both facts inform how Colombo's cases should be plotted (slow-building, witness-dependent, vulnerable to intimidation) versus a faster US-style procedural rhythm.

---

# 19. Travel, Borders & the Wider World

Naples' position within the EU (open borders with most of continental Europe, a functioning international airport, port connections to North Africa and the wider Mediterranean) is available as a plot resource for smuggling-route, extradition-risk, or exile storylines in Season 2–3 without requiring new world-building — flagged here as a resource for future Season Bible development rather than committed Season 1 canon.

# 20. The Ritual Year

The in-world calendar is built around Catholic liturgical and Neapolitan civic ritual, giving the Season Bible natural episode-anchoring events:

| Occasion | Narrative Use |
|---|---|
| **Easter (Pasqua)** | Family-gathering pressure point; strong candidate for an early-season episode anchor given its emphasis on renewal/rupture themes |
| **Feast of San Gennaro** (Naples' patron saint) | A real, major Neapolitan civic-religious event; strong candidate for a mid-season episode where both families' public presence at the same event creates unavoidable proximity and tension |
| **A wedding** (family TBD in Season Bible) | Classic mid-season "both families in one room under truce-etiquette" structural device |
| **A funeral** (tied to the inciting incident or its consequences) | Structurally likely season-defining event; see Story Bible §6 |
| **Christmas (Natale)** | Season-ending or season-opening anchor candidate, given its family-centric weight |

---

# 21. Seasonal & Atmospheric Design Notes

Naples' Mediterranean climate — hot, bright summers and milder, wetter winters — gives the production a natural tool for tonal shift across a season: proposed design note that Season 1 move visually from high-summer brightness (Story Bible §3's "hard Mediterranean sun") in its early episodes toward greyer, wetter, more shadowed conditions by the finale, mirroring the Peace's collapse without requiring on-the-nose dialogue to signal the tonal shift.

# 23. Background Texture & Crowd World

Naples' street-level texture — market vendors, elderly residents on balconies, children playing in courtyards, motorini traffic — should be treated as a consistent, specific, recurring "world" rather than generic extras work; per Story Bible §3, the production design mandate is specificity over "Italian" as a generic aesthetic category, and this extends to background performance and crowd blocking.

---

# 24. Naples Beyond the Story — What We Don't Show

Consistent with Story Bible §2's positioning against political-thriller comparables like *Suburra*, this World Bible deliberately does not expand into a full depiction of Naples' broader political apparatus, national government, or international criminal networks beyond what is needed to service the Ranieri/Esposito story — keeping the show's focus, per its core positioning, on one family's fracture rather than a citywide or national panorama.
