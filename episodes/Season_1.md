# VESUVIO: Blood of Naples — Season 1 Episode Guide

*10 episodes, ~50 minutes each*

---

### Episode 1 — "'A Pace" ("The Peace")
Ten years of uneasy truce hold the Naples underworld together. On the
anniversary of the Peace, a small, deniable provocation from Clan
Esposito sets off a chain of suspicion inside the Ranieri family. We meet
Salvatore, Lucia, Marco, and the fragile order they've built — and the
first crack in it.

### Episode 2 — "'O Sanghe" ("The Blood")
A Ranieri associate is found dead under circumstances that could be
accident, warning, or declaration of war. Marco is sent to investigate
quietly; Elena Ferraro opens a file. The audience meets Antonio Esposito
properly for the first time.

### Episode 3 — "'O Silenzio" ("The Silence")
Salvatore forbids retaliation, insisting the incident was isolated. Lucia
begins working her own back channels, unconvinced. Don Pietro hears a
confession that puts him in an impossible position.

### Episode 4 — "'O Rispetto" ("Respect")
A public insult forces Salvatore's hand — he must respond or lose
standing. Marco is given his first major test of loyalty, executing an
order he privately disagrees with. Judge Conte's old debt to the family
is hinted at for the first time.

### Episode 5 — "'A Legge" ("The Law")
Elena's investigation gains real traction after a witness comes forward.
Conte is pressured — by the family and by his own conscience — to slow
the case down. Antonio, sensing weakness, escalates.

### Episode 6 — "Sanghe Friddo" ("Cold Blood")
Midpoint episode. A direct confrontation between Ranieri and Esposito
territory turns violent for the first time in a decade. The Peace is
now, functionally, broken — though no one will say so out loud yet.

### Episode 7 — "'O Putere" ("Power")
Salvatore's authority is openly questioned within his own family for the
first time. Marco and Lucia begin operating in concert, not always with
Salvatore's knowledge. Antonio makes his boldest move yet.

### Episode 8 — "Nu Sgarro" ("No Mistakes")
A mistake — human, not strategic — threatens to expose the family's
institutional protection. Elena and Conte's alliance is tested. Don
Pietro is asked, directly, to choose a side.

### Episode 9 — "'A Morte Se Sconta" ("Death Is Paid For")
The betrayal hinted at since the pilot is finally revealed. Alliances the
audience trusted are upended. Marco is forced into the position his
entire arc has been building toward.

### Episode 10 — "Vesuvio"
Season finale. The Peace collapses completely. Salvatore, Lucia, and
Marco each make a defining choice about what kind of power they're
willing to hold onto — and what they're willing to lose to keep it. The
mountain, dormant all season, finally "erupts" — figuratively and, in the
show's closing image, almost literally.

---

**Season 2 (planned):** Open war between the families under intensified
institutional pressure, as Marco assumes real authority for the first
time.

**Season 3 (planned):** Resolution of the founding generation's arc and
handover of power to their successors.
