# VESUVIO: BLOOD OF NAPLES

## Episode 1: "'A Pace" ("The Peace")

### Written by Ciprian Stefan Plesca

*Pilot script — Act One (sample pages). Full pilot available on request.*

---

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. NAPLES SKYLINE — NIGHT**

Mount Vesuvius sits black against a bruised sky. Below it, the city
sprawls in a thousand scattered lights — apartment blocks, church domes,
the dark line of the sea.

A distant SIREN. A dog barks. Somewhere, church bells mark the hour.

SUPER: *NAPLES. TEN YEARS AFTER THE PEACE.*

**CUT TO:**

**INT. RIONE — STAIRWELL — NIGHT**

Cramped, concrete, lit by a single bare bulb. MARCO RANIERI (34, coiled
energy under a calm surface) climbs fast, two steps at a time. He's not
running — he's *late*.

**MARCO**
*(under his breath)*
Non ce sta tiempo. Non ce sta tiempo.
*(There's no time. There's no time.)*

He reaches a door, raises a fist to knock — stops. Breathes. Composes
himself completely. Knocks once, calm.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. RANIERI VILLA — DINING ROOM — CONTINUOUS**

A long table. Old money, not new. DON SALVATORE RANIERI (58, still,
watchful) sits at its head. DONNA LUCIA RANIERI (55, elegant, missing
nothing) pours wine like she's done it a thousand times without ever
once being just a hostess.

Marco enters. Salvatore doesn't look up.

**SALVATORE**
Ten years ago tonight, guagliò. You know what tonight is.

**MARCO**
I know what tonight is.

**SALVATORE**
*(finally looking up)*
Then you know why you don't come through that door late.

**LUCIA**
*(smooth, defusing)*
He's here. Sit. Eat. The dead don't mind waiting a few more minutes,
Salvatore.

A beat. Salvatore almost smiles. Marco sits.

**SALVATORE**
*(raising his glass)*
'A Pace.

**LUCIA / MARCO**
*(together)*
'A Pace.

They drink. Outside the window — far off, easy to miss — a faint red
line glows on the dark shape of the volcano. Nobody at the table sees it.
The audience does.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. PORT OF NAPLES — CONTAINER YARD — NIGHT**

Floodlights. Containers stacked like a maze. A SHIPPING FOREMAN
(50s, nervous) walks fast beside ANTONIO ESPOSITO (32, magnetic,
barely-contained energy).

**ANTONIO**
Uagliù, tell me again — whose name is on this contract?

**FOREMAN**
Ranieri's. Same as it's been for—

**ANTONIO**
*(cutting him off, calm — which is worse)*
Ten years. I know how long. My father knows how long. Everyone in this
city knows how long.

He stops walking. Looks up at a container being lowered by crane —
watches it a beat too long.

**ANTONIO (CONT'D)**
Tonight, that changes. Just a little. Just enough that they notice.

**FOREMAN**
*(quiet, scared)*
Antò... this breaks the Peace.

**ANTONIO**
*(a real smile — the scary kind)*
No. It just moves the line. There's a difference.
*(beat)*
Or there used to be.

He nods once. Off-screen, machinery grinds to a sudden, wrong stop.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. RANIERI VILLA — DINING ROOM — LATER**

The meal continues, warm, almost normal. Marco's phone buzzes once on the
table. He glances at it. His face doesn't change — but Lucia notices
that it doesn't change, which tells her everything.

**LUCIA**
*(quiet, only to Marco)*
Whatever that is, it can wait until dessert.

**MARCO**
*(matching her tone)*
It already didn't.

Salvatore, oblivious, raises his glass again, toasting something only he
can see.

**SALVATORE**
Ten years of peace. Let's make it twenty.

Marco raises his glass. Doesn't drink yet.

**MARCO**
*(to himself, almost)*
'O silenzio parla chiaro.
*(Silence speaks clearly.)*

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE (SAMPLE).**

## Full Episode Outline (Acts Two–Four)

**ACT TWO:** Marco is dispatched to assess the port incident quietly, per
Salvatore's orders — no retaliation, only information. He crosses paths
with Elena Ferraro for the first time, unaware she's already building a
file on his family. Antonio, meanwhile, tests how far his father is
willing to let him push.

**ACT THREE:** Marco reports back with more questions than answers.
Salvatore insists on treating the incident as isolated; Lucia privately
disagrees and begins her own inquiry. Don Pietro receives a confession
that hints the "accident" at the port was no accident at all.

**ACT FOUR:** The episode closes on parallel images — Salvatore alone on
the villa balcony, looking toward Vesuvius; Antonio watching the same
mountain from the port; Elena pinning the first thread of her case to a
board. The Peace is still standing. Everyone at the table can feel it
starting to lean.

**END OF EPISODE ONE.**
