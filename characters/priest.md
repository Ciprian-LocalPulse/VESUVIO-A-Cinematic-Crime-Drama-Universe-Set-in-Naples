# Don Pietro — "The Priest"

## Biography
49, parish priest of the Rione's church for over fifteen years. Don
Pietro baptizes the family's children, buries its dead, and hears
confessions he can never repeat — placing him, structurally, closer to
the family's secrets than anyone outside it.

## Psychological Profile
Don Pietro is the show's living embodiment of its central moral question:
can genuine faith coexist with willful blindness? He is not cynical or
corrupt in the way Conte is — he believes, sincerely, in grace and
forgiveness, which is exactly what makes him useful to a family that
needs both.

## Motivations
- Protect his parish and community, which depends materially on the
  family's "charity."
- Preserve a genuine, uncorrupted relationship with faith, even inside a
  compromised position.
- Intervene, quietly, wherever he believes he can save someone without
  breaking his vows.

## Fears
- That his silence, however sacred in form, is complicity in substance.
- Losing the trust of a community that needs him to be both untouchable
  and human.

## Relationships
- **Lucia (Wife)** — his closest confidante within the family, and the
  person most likely to test the limits of what he'll stay silent about.
- **Marco (Underboss)** — a former altar boy whose current path troubles
  him personally, not just morally.

## Evolution
Don Pietro spends the season absorbing the emotional and moral fallout of
the collapsing Peace — confessions, funerals, private counsel — building
to a finale moment where his silence becomes an active choice rather than
a passive one.

## Casting Suggestions
An actor in their late 40s with a warm, grounded presence — someone able
to hold moral seriousness without sanctimony, credible as a genuine
spiritual anchor rather than a genre stock character.
