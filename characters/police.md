# Commissario Elena Ferraro — "The Police"

## Biography
41, a career investigator with the Naples anti-mafia division (DDA-
adjacent unit), transferred in from northern Italy five years ago
specifically to work organized-crime cases. An outsider in a city whose
institutional relationships she doesn't yet fully understand — and
doesn't trust once she does.

## Psychological Profile
Elena is the show's institutional conscience, but the series is careful
not to make her simplistically heroic — her single-minded pursuit of the
Ranieri family repeatedly costs innocent people around her, and she knows
it. Her arc interrogates whether "the law" in this world is really a
counterforce to the Camorra, or just another competing power structure.

## Motivations
- Build a case that actually holds — not just against street-level
  affiliates, but the family's institutional protection.
- Prove that she wasn't sent to Naples to fail quietly.
- Personal: an unresolved connection to a prior case elsewhere in Italy
  that makes this investigation personal in ways she won't admit.

## Fears
- Becoming compromised, like every investigator before her in this city.
- That the institutions she serves are as unwilling to change as the
  family she's chasing.

## Relationships
- **Salvatore (Boss)** — the case she's building her career on.
- **Judge (see `judge.md`)** — an uneasy professional alliance strained by
  institutional politics.

## Evolution
Elena begins the season as an outsider gathering evidence from a
distance. As the Peace collapses and violence becomes visible, she's
forced into direct, dangerous proximity to the family she's investigating
— ending the season with leverage she's not yet sure how to use.

## Casting Suggestions
An actress in her early-to-mid 40s able to project quiet authority and
moral ambiguity — someone credible as both a rigorous investigator and a
flawed, isolated person.
