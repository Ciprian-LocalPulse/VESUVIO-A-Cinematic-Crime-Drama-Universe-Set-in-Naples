# VESUVIO: Blood of Naples
## Moodbook — Visual & Sonic Identity

---

## 1. Cinematography

- **Night-dominant photography.** The majority of key dramatic scenes are
  lit and shot at night or in low, practical light — streetlamps,
  interiors, single sources — reinforcing the show's theme of things kept
  hidden.
- **Wide establishing shots of Vesuvius** recur as punctuation between
  scenes, always static, always distant — the mountain never moves,
  everything around it does.
- **Handheld intimacy indoors, locked-off control outdoors** — the
  camera mirrors the difference between the family's private,
  emotionally volatile world and its careful public presentation.
- **High contrast, desaturated grade** — blacks are deep, skin tones stay
  warm and human against a cool, muted environment.

## 2. Costume & Styling

- **The family:** tailored but never flashy — old money restraint, not
  gangster-genre excess. Salvatore in muted greys and navy; Lucia in
  structured, elegant pieces that read as armor.
- **The rising generation (Marco, Antonio):** sharper, more modern
  silhouettes — a visible generational break from their elders' more
  traditional style.
- **Street level:** grounded, working-class realism — no stylization for
  its own sake.

## 3. Architecture & Location Texture

- Contrast between Naples' Baroque historic center (warm stone, ornate
  detail) and the dense concrete *rioni* (utilitarian, weathered,
  intensely lived-in).
- The Ranieri villa: old-world interiors — dark wood, heavy fabric,
  religious iconography — deliberately at odds with the modern criminal
  economy that funds it.
- The port: industrial scale, floodlit, cold — the show's primary
  "business" location, visually opposed to the villa's warmth.

## 4. Naples as Character

Naples is shot as a city of contradictions held in the same frame:
tourists and territory, sunlight and shadow, sacred and profane — often
literally, via church processions passing streets that, hours later,
become crime scenes.

## 5. Color Palette

| Palette Role | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary | Charcoal, near-black navy, deep slate |
| Secondary | Warm stone / sun-bleached ochre (historic center exteriors) |
| Accent | Muted gold — reserved for ritual, power, and key emotional beats |
| Blood | Deep, desaturated red — used sparingly, never glorified |

See [`/assets/cover_art.jpg`](../assets/cover_art.jpg) for the key art
built directly around this palette.

## 6. Sonic Identity

The show's sound identity is anchored by its original Neapolitan-dialect
theme track — dark melodic trap blended with traditional instrumentation
cues (bells, sub-bass, industrial percussion) — establishing a sonic
signature distinct from typical string-driven crime-drama scoring. See
[`/docs/LYRICS_BILINGUAL.md`](LYRICS_BILINGUAL.md) and
[`/assets/track_final.mp3`](../assets/track_final.mp3).

## 7. Inspirations

For tonal and craft reference only (not derivative intent):

- Multi-generational, character-first crime family sagas
- European regional-language prestige drama
- Naturalistic, natural-light-driven cinematography in contemporary
  European film
